Blue Brain Project

One of the keys towards understanding how the brain works as a whole is visualisation of how the individual cells function.
The Blue Brain Project has made major efforts to create morphologically accurate neurons to simulate sub-cellular and electrical activities, e.g. molecular simulations of neuron biochemistry or multi-scale simulations of neuronal function. In combination with ‘global illumination’, which uses light, shadow, and depth of field effects to simulate photo-realistic images, ray-tracing makes it easier to visualise how the neurons function.
